## STANDARD DEDUCTIONS 2025
| Filing Status | Amount |
|---------------|--------|
| Single | $14,800 |
| Married Filing Jointly (MFJ) | $29,600 |
| Married Filing Separately (MFS) | $14,800 |
| Head of Household (HOH) | $22,200 |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse (QSS) | $29,600 |

### Additional Standard Deduction (Age 65+ or Blind)
| Filing Status | Per Condition |
|---------------|---------------|
| Single or HOH | +$1,850 |
| Married (MFJ/MFS/QSS) | +$1,500 |

**Note:** Each person can have up to 2 conditions (65+ AND blind).
- Example: MFJ couple, both 65+ = $29,600 + $1,500 + $1,500 = $32,600
- Example: Single, 65+ AND blind = $14,800 + $1,850 + $1,850 = $18,500

**Age 65+ Rule:** You are considered 65 on the day BEFORE your 65th birthday.
- For 2025: Born BEFORE January 2, 1961 = Age 65+

## CHILD TAX CREDIT (CTC) 2025
| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Credit per qualifying child | $2,000 |
| Maximum refundable (ACTC) | $1,700 per child |
| Earned income threshold | $2,500 |

### Qualifying Child Requirements:
1. Under age 17 at end of tax year (born after Dec 31, 2008)
2. Your son, daughter, stepchild, foster child, sibling, or descendant
3. Lived with you for more than half the year
4. Did not provide more than half of their own support
5. U.S. citizen, U.S. national, or U.S. resident alien
6. Has a valid Social Security Number (SSN)

### Phase-out Thresholds:
| Filing Status | Phase-out Begins | Reduction |
|---------------|------------------|-----------|
| Married Filing Jointly | $400,000 | $50 per $1,000 over |
| All Other Filers | $200,000 | $50 per $1,000 over |

### Form 1040 Lines:
- Line 19: Child Tax Credit (non-refundable portion)
- Line 28: Additional Child Tax Credit (refundable portion)
- Schedule 8812: Calculate CTC and ACTC

## CREDIT FOR OTHER DEPENDENTS (ODC) 2025
| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Credit per qualifying dependent | $500 |
| Refundable? | NO (non-refundable) |

### Who Qualifies:
- Dependents who do NOT qualify for Child Tax Credit
- Children age 17 or older
- Dependent parents
- Other relatives who qualify as dependents

## EARNED INCOME TAX CREDIT (EITC) 2025
| Children | Max Credit | Single/HOH Limit | MFJ Limit | Investment Limit |
|----------|------------|------------------|-----------|------------------|
| 0 | $649 | $18,591 | $25,511 | $11,600 |
| 1 | $4,328 | $49,084 | $56,004 | $11,600 |
| 2 | $7,152 | $55,768 | $62,688 | $11,600 |
| 3+ | $8,046 | $59,899 | $66,819 | $11,600 |

### EITC Requirements:
1. Must have earned income (wages, self-employment)
2. Investment income must be $11,600 or less
3. Must be U.S. citizen or resident alien all year
4. Cannot file as Married Filing Separately
5. Cannot be a qualifying child of another person
6. If no children: Must be age 25-64 at end of year

### Qualifying Child for EITC:
- Under age 19 (or under 24 if full-time student)
- Lived with you in US for more than half the year
- Has valid Social Security Number

## SELF-EMPLOYMENT TAX 2025
- Rate: 15.3% (12.4% SS + 2.9% Medicare)
- Applied to: 92.35% of net self-employment income
- Deduction: 50% of SE tax is deductible from income
- Minimum threshold: $400 net SE income

## FORM 1040 LINE REFERENCE

### Income (Lines 1-9)
- Line 1: Wages, salaries, tips (W-2)
- Line 2: Interest income (1099-INT)
- Line 3: Dividend income (1099-DIV)
- Line 4: IRA/pension distributions (1099-R)
- Line 5: Annuities
- Line 6: Social Security benefits (SSA-1099)
- Line 7: Capital gains (Schedule D)
- Line 8: Other income (Schedule 1)
- Line 9: TOTAL INCOME

### AGI and Deductions (Lines 10-15)
- Line 10: Adjustments (Schedule 1)
- Line 11: ADJUSTED GROSS INCOME (AGI)
- Line 12: Standard or Itemized deduction
- Line 14: Total deductions
- Line 15: TAXABLE INCOME

### Tax and Credits (Lines 16-24)
- Line 16: Tax (from Tax Tables)
- Line 18: Total tax before credits
- Line 19: Child Tax Credit + ODC
- Line 22: Tax after credits
- Line 24: TOTAL TAX

### Payments (Lines 25-33)
- Line 25: Federal withholding (W-2, 1099)
- Line 26: Estimated payments
- Line 27: EITC
- Line 28: Additional Child Tax Credit (ACTC)
- Line 33: TOTAL PAYMENTS

### Refund or Owed (Lines 34-37)
- Line 34: REFUND (if payments > tax)
- Line 37: AMOUNT OWED (if tax > payments)

## COMMON QUESTIONS

Q: What is the 2025 standard deduction for single filers?
A: $14,800

Q: What is the 2025 standard deduction for married filing jointly?
A: $29,600

Q: What is the additional deduction for taxpayers 65 or older?
A: Single/HOH: $1,850 per condition. Married: $1,500 per condition.

Q: How do I know if I'm 65+ for 2025 taxes?
A: You're considered 65 on the day before your 65th birthday. For 2025: Born before January 2, 1961.

Q: What is the Child Tax Credit amount?
A: $2,000 per qualifying child under age 17.

Q: How much of CTC is refundable?
A: Up to $1,700 per child as Additional Child Tax Credit (ACTC).

Q: What is the Other Dependents Credit?
A: $500 per qualifying dependent age 17 or older. Non-refundable.

Q: What is the maximum EITC for 3 children?
A: $8,046

Q: What are the IRA contribution limits for 2025?
A: $7,000 (under 50) or $8,000 (50+) per person.

Q: What are the HSA contribution limits for 2025?
A: $4,300 (self-only) or $8,550 (family).

Q: What are the 401(k) contribution limits for 2025?
A: $23,500 base, +$7,500 catch-up (age 50+), +$11,250 super catch-up (age 60-63).
